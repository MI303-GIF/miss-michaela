What is Thrombopoiesis

Regulated by a hormone, thrombopoietin Hormone thought to be produced by the liver. Platelets formed by pinching off of the cytoplasm of megakaryocytes. After acute thrombocytopenia 3-4 day are required to replenish the loss.

PLATELET BIOCHEMISTRY-PROTEINS

1.Thrombostenin- An actomyosin –like protein, responsible for clot retraction.

2.Fibrinogen- found in α-granules and on surface

3.Platelet factor 4- Protein with anti-heparin activity.

4.PF3- a lipoprotein –commonly referred to as phospholipid or cephalin –important in generation of plasma thromboplastin.

5.Platelet Derived Growth Factor- potent mitogen required for the proliferation of CT cells
